My research interests include neighborhood effects, urban network models, spatial equilibrium models, agglomeration economies, spatial trade dynamics, innovation across space, local public finance, zoning, urban economic history, and related spatial and urban topics. Below are projects that I am actively working on. These began as thesis topics, and I am continuing to develop them with the goal of getting them to a state where they can be published. Please feel free to reach out with any questions.

## Working Papers

<div class="research-entry">
  <h3><a href="/assets/pdf/occupation_subsector_complementarity.pdf" target="_blank">Occupation-Subsector Complementarity on Establishment-Level Growth</a></h3>
  <p class="research-meta">Working paper</p>
  <p>
    This paper studies how complementarities between occupations and subsectors shape establishment-level growth.
    The project develops measures of specialization within establishments and examines how those complementarities
    relate to firm performance, local agglomeration forces, and broader urban structure.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="research-entry">
  <h3><a href="/assets/pdf/agglomeration_premium_polycentric_cities.pdf" target="_blank">Agglomeration Premium in Polycentric Cities</a></h3>
  <p class="research-meta">Working paper</p>
  <p>
    This paper examines how agglomeration advantages operate in polycentric urban environments, with particular
    attention to how specialization, density, and spatial structure interact across subcenters within large metropolitan areas.
  </p>
</div>
